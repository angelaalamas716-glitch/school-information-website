School History

One Cainta College (OCC) is the official local government college of the Municipality of Cainta, Rizal. It was established to provide accessible, affordable, and quality higher education to the residents of Cainta while producing graduates who are globally competitive, socially responsible, and prepared to contribute to the development of their community and the country.

Founding and Legal Basis

The college was formally created through Municipal Ordinance No. 2016-021, entitled "An Ordinance Establishing a Local College in the Municipality of Cainta to be Known as One Cainta College." This ordinance was approved by the Sangguniang Bayan of Cainta on August 30, 2016. The establishment of the college was initiated by then Mayor J. Keith P. Nieto, with the support of the local government and in coordination with the Commission on Higher Education (CHED).

Early Development

To prepare for the opening of the institution, the local government passed Ordinance No. 2017-002, which created administrative and academic positions necessary for the operation of the college. Later, Ordinance No. 2018-009 amended the original charter to incorporate recommendations from CHED during its institutional evaluation. In addition, Ordinance No. 2018-010 assigned the perpetual use of the college's campus and facilities located in Hunters Avenue, Barangay San Juan, Cainta, Rizal, ensuring that the institution had a permanent location for its operations.

CHED Recognition and Founding Programs

A significant milestone in the college's history was achieved on October 22, 2018, when One Cainta College received its Certificate of Recognition as a Higher Education Institution (HEI) from CHED through CHED Commission En Banc Resolution No. 473-2018. This recognition authorized the college to operate as an official higher education institution and to offer bachelor's degree programs beginning in the 2019–2020 academic year. Initially, OCC offered three undergraduate programs:

  • Bachelor of Science in Entrepreneurship (BSE)
  • Bachelor of Science in Information Systems (BSIS)
  • Bachelor in Technical-Vocational Teacher Education (BTVTEd)

Scholarship and Financial Assistance

As the college continued to grow, it also qualified for government scholarship programs under the Universal Access to Quality Tertiary Education Act (RA 10931) through the Unified Student Financial Assistance System for Tertiary Education (UniFAST). Eligible students became beneficiaries of programs such as the Tertiary Education Subsidy (TES) and the Tulong Dunong Program (TDP), helping many students pursue higher education with reduced financial burden.

Program Certification

In 2021, One Cainta College further strengthened its commitment to academic excellence by obtaining Certificates of Program Compliance (COPC) from CHED for its three bachelor's degree programs. This certification confirmed that the programs met the national standards and policies required for quality higher education in the Philippines.

OCC Today

Today, One Cainta College continues to fulfill its mission of providing quality, relevant, and accessible education. Guided by its vision of becoming a recognized and reputable institution of higher learning, OCC promotes excellence in instruction, research, community engagement, and partnerships while preparing students to become competent professionals, responsible citizens, and future leaders who will contribute to the progress of Cainta, the nation, and the global community.
